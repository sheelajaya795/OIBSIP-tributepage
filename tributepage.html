Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi

well-known as a freedom fighter and the father of our nation.

Here's a time line of Dr. Borlaug's life:

  • 1869 - born in Porbandar, Kathiawar, now known as Gujarat.
  • 1876 - Mahatma Gandhi was brought up in India near Rajkot, where he did most of his studies
  • 1888 - Gandhi went to London, leaving his wife and son behind, to pursue his degree in law.
  • 1893 - Gandhi went to South Africa to work and found that there was a lot of prejudice towards Indians.
  • 1915 - When Gandhi returned to India he led campaigns to improve the lives of India’s peasants, farmers and labourers
  • 1919 - Gandhi became involved in Indian politics and aligned himself with the Indian National Congress.
  • 1921 - He became leader of the Congress
  • 1934 - but chose to retire from the party when his colleagues failed to fully embrace satyagraha.
  • 1942 - However, Gandhi remained committed to the fight for independence and was imprisoned
  • 1944 - until for demanding British withdrawal from India. .
  • 1947 - Although Gandhi welcomed Britain’s exit, he wanted to maintain a united India where the Hindu, Muslim and Sikh peoples could co-exist. Therefore when Partition happened , Gandhi did not participate in any official celebrations and instead visited areas that were greatly affected by the ensuing violence.
  • 1948 - While many people supported Gandhi and his teachings, some Hindus believed that he was too supportive of Pakistan and Indian Muslims. Such views led militant Hindu nationalist Nathuram Godse to assassinate Gandhi at an inter-faith prayer meeting in Delhi on 30 January
